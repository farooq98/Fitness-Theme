Our Coaches

Prime has a strong line-up of foreign and local coaches to provide our gymnasts with a varied, inspiring and truly international environment. Coaches have to meet stringent selection criteria before they are employed. Our team of passionate and highly experienced and qualified coaches have trained national teams and world champion and Olympic qualifiers. We provide numerous opportunities and offer our full support to our coaches to further their education. Prime has had the opportunity to bring in High Performance Coaches from the UK, Russia, China and the USA to run training camps for the benefit of our gymnasts.

Andrey

Born in Russia, Andrey was a member of the Junior National Gymnastics Team of USSR. With a Master’s degree in Sport USSR, he began working as a professional gymnastics coach in Poland, representing Poland at the World Gymnastics Championship in Japan and in two European Gymnastics Championships. He also holds a Bachelor’s degree of Sports and Physical Culture from St. Petersburg Russia. During his career, he produced various elite gymnasts and brought them among others to World Championships, European Championships, US National Championships and US Visa Championships

Rimma

Rimma comes to Prime from the Sunshine Coast in Queensland Australia. She has a Bachelor's degree in Physical Education and Sport and has achieved the level of Master of Sport in Russia. She was a member of the Russian National Team in Women's Gymnastics. Rimma is a high level Choreographer and has spent a significant amount of time working with members of the Japanese National Gymnastics Team. She has been coaching high level WAG since 1993 and has coached in Russia, Japan, USA and most recently Australia. Gymnasts training under her guidance have achieved superior results in national and international competitions throughout the world. Outside the gym, Rimma enjoys spending time with her family and friends and travelling.

James

James is a pioneer gymnastics coach of Prime. He is amazed at Prime’s growth over the years and we are proud to have him lead the boys’ team. He brings with him 34 years of coaching experience and holds a Master’s degree from Beijing Sports University. He has decided to sink his roots in Singapore and became a citizen in 2002. James enjoys travelling and spending time with his family when he is not coaching

Coach Xu

Coach Xu Heping comes with more than 40 years of coaching experience. He has multiple degrees in higher sports training from Shanghai University of Sport and is currently affiliated with China’s National Programme. He was a High Performance Coach at An Hui Provisional Gymnastics Team, China. Coach Xu has produced numerous high performance gymnasts, including an Olympian. He then moved to Singapore and was coaching at Singapore Gymnastics for 2 years before joining Prime. A former fishing enthusiast, Coach Xu enjoys tea-tasting, walks with his wife, watching Chinese history films and listening to traditional Chinese music

Brydon

As a gymnast, Brydon was a former MAG National team member of the Philippines, representing the country from 1996-2006. He was a consistent SEA Games medallist and competed in the World Gymnastics Championships. Brydon coached in Shanghai for 2 years prior to joining Prime for 7 years when he first arrived in Singapore. During his former stint with Prime, he was the Head Coach of St. Nicholas Girls' School (Primary and Secondary). Brydon has also coached and developed gymnasts who were selected as members of the Singapore National Team at Singapore Gymnastics. He has FIG Level 3 coaching certification and is a Singapore Technical Level 2 coach accredited by Sport Singapore. Brydon enjoys travelling and watching movies when he is not coaching.

Casie

Casie was a USA gymnast Level 10. She holds a Director Certification from USA Gymnastics, is Singapore Gymnastics National Coaches Accreditation Programme (NCAP) Level 2 certified and a Positive Coaching Alliance certification holder. She has coached in the US, Bermuda and China before immigrating to Singapore in 2010. As a founding member of International Handstand Day she helped spearhead international recognition for handstand enthusiasts all over the globe. Outside of the gym, she is the Singapore Director for SheWorx, a global collective of female entrepreneurs redefining leadership. In her spare time she enjoys reading, coding and helping people achieve their goals.

Rannie

Ronnie started as a gymnast at the age of 10. He was selected as a member of the Men’s Artistic National Team of the Gymnastics Association of the Philippines to represent the Philippines from 1996 to 2006. Ronnie has over 18 years of experience coaching in the Philippines, China (Beijing and Shanghai) and Singapore. Throughout his coaching career, Ronnie has successfully led WAG and MAG teams to various competitions and training camps in the South East Asian region. He holds a MAG Level 1 FIG Coaching certificate and is a Certified Standard First Aider and CPR Provider. Ronnie is a passionate mountain biker and has biked in various mountain bike trails in Singapore and in the region.

Yang Yang

Yang Yong holds a Bachelor of Arts in Gymnastics from the Shandong Province Institute of Physical Education in China. As a gymnast, he represented his province Shandong and won, among others, the 1995 Pegasus Cup, finishing 3rd in the 2002 Red Cup National Gymnastic Championship and qualifying for the 14th Asian Games. He was awarded the Sportsmanship Award by the National Gymnastics Championship in 2004. Arriving in Singapore in 2010, he became a full time coach in local gymnastic clubs where his gymnasts performed well at national competitions. He holds Singapore Gymnastics NCAP Level 1 certification and is a registered coach with Sports Singapore. Yang Yong plays tennis, listens to music and enjoys watching movies in his spare time.

Madam Xu

Madam Xu Zhihong holds a Diploma in Physical Education majoring in Gymnastics from the Shanghai Institute of Physical Education. She represented Jiangxi Province in many national and international competitions. She has been a high performance coach in China since 1985 and was appointed a national judge. Madam Xu Zhihong started coaching in Singapore in 2002 in schools and clubs and her gymnasts have achieved good results in Inter-school competitions. In her spare time, Madam Xu is a fan of musical theatre and ballet performances. She loves to dance, play table-tennis and enjoys swimming.

Tina

Tina successfully competed in artistic gymnastics competitions in the South East Asian region. As a member of the Philippines Aerobics Gymnastics Team, she competed in the Asian Indoor Games 2007 Macau, FIG Aero Gymnastics World Series 2008 and National Ranking Philippines. As a member of Team Pilipinas Cheerleading/CCP Bobcats, she competed in the World Cup Cheer and Dance in Australia 2009/2010, National Cheerleading Championships 2010 Philippines and SEACO 2011 Singapore. Tina is also a trained dancer in hip hop, basic ballet, Jazz and contemporary dance. She arrived in Singapore in 2011 and coached at a gymnastics club for 2 years before joining Prime. She is a certified Cheerleading Association of Singapore Coach CAT A, AACCA Safety Coach, Singapore Gymnastics Level 1/2 Judge, Gymnastics Australia WAG Beginner Judge, NCAP GG Level 1, FIG Academy Level 1 and FIG Academy Level 2. Outside the gym, Tina enjoys travelling, adventure sports, cooking and spending time with family and friends.

Karen

Karen represented Singapore as a young gymnast. She was a pioneer National Gymnast and a Gold Medallist at the South East Asian Games (SEA Games) in 1979. A well-regarded coach in gymnastics and other fitness training classes, Karen comes with many years of coaching experience. She has coached with Prime for 4 years, juggling her coaching responsibilities with family commitments. For leisure, Karen enjoys playing table tennis, badminton and Chinese chess.

Ying

Born in China, Ying started Gymnastics at the age of 4. She holds a Bachelor degree from Beijing Sports university and she has participated in national competitions on behalf of her team at many occasions.
She came to work in Singapore as a gymnastics coach in 2013. In the past 5 years, her athletes have participated in the Singapore national competition and the inter-school competition on behalf of the school and the club, and have achieved excellent results. At the same time she has obtained the certificate of the Level 1 coach and the level 2 judge of Singapore. Ying loves swimming and traveling.

Our General Manager

Soheila

Soheila has had over 10 years of management and teaching experience in a similar industry. She holds a bachelor degree in Engineering from Leeds Metropolitan University in the UK. Following a successful career in engineering Soheila moved to Singapore in 1993 and after a period of teaching at Ngee Ann polytechnic, in 2002 she embarked on a venture closer to her childhood passion for dance and fitness, and she devised a way to combine her analytical skills and teaching experience to create series of dance/fitness programs aimed at ladies and children. Prior to joining Prime Gymnastics, Soheila was the Managing Director of a dance studio and her programs attracted hundreds of students from all ages and all walks of life. She has choreographed numerous performances including numbers staged in the Chingay Parade and Esplanade as well as many charity events.
